# AB-730 Pitfalls and Gotchas

## 1. Web Copilot does NOT see your work files

The free Microsoft Copilot searches the web. Only **Microsoft 365 Copilot** can read your tenant's emails, files, and chats.

## 2. M365 Copilot is licensed separately

Having an M365 license is NOT enough. You need a per-user **Microsoft 365 Copilot** add-on license.

## 3. Copilot Pro is consumer-only

Microsoft Copilot Pro adds Copilot to consumer Office (M365 Personal/Family). It does NOT add Copilot to a business tenant - that requires M365 Copilot.

## 4. Excel needs structured tables

Copilot in Excel requires data in a **table** (Insert > Table). Raw cells often won't work.

## 5. Hallucinations happen in summaries too

Even when summarizing your own document, Copilot can drop or distort facts.

## 6. Sensitivity labels DO propagate

If you ask Copilot to summarize a Confidential document, the output inherits the Confidential label.

## 7. Citations are clickable - and you should click them

Don't trust a citation just because it exists. Click and verify.

## 8. Copilot does NOT replace expertise

Legal, medical, financial - you still need an expert review.

## 9. Free Copilot has rate limits

Daily image-generation "boost" limits and throttling possible.

## 10. Don't paste regulated data into free Copilot

PHI, PCI, classified - never. Use a tool with the right compliance posture.

## 11. Conversation memory is per-chat

Copilot remembers within a chat, not across chats.

## 12. Generic prompts produce generic output

If the answer was bland, the prompt was bland. Apply GCSE.

## 13. AI-generated images can be biased

Without prompt guardrails, generated images may default to non-inclusive depictions.

## 14. Copilot Studio is for makers, not end users

Building a custom Copilot agent is Copilot Studio territory.

## 15. M365 Copilot data is NOT used to train foundation models

Microsoft does not use tenant data to train its base models.
