<section class="fc-section" data-fc-title="Generative AI Fundamentals">

<div class="flashcard"><div class="fc-q">What is a hallucination?<div class="fc-a"><strong>An AI output that sounds confident but is factually wrong.</strong></div></div></div>

<div class="flashcard"><div class="fc-q">Difference between Microsoft Copilot (free) and Microsoft 365 Copilot?<div class="fc-a"><strong>Free</strong>: web-grounded via Bing. <strong>M365</strong>: tenant-grounded via Microsoft Graph (your emails, files, chats). M365 requires a paid per-user license.</div></div></div>

<div class="flashcard"><div class="fc-q">Microsoft's six responsible AI principles?<div class="fc-a"><strong>Fairness, Reliability and safety, Privacy and security, Inclusiveness, Transparency, Accountability.</strong></div></div></div>

<div class="flashcard"><div class="fc-q">Which principle says users must know they are interacting with AI?<div class="fc-a"><strong>Transparency.</strong></div></div></div>

<div class="flashcard"><div class="fc-q">Three ways to mitigate hallucinations?<div class="fc-a"><strong>1. Use grounded tools (M365 Copilot). 2. Ask for citations. 3. Cross-check with primary source.</strong></div></div></div>

</section>
